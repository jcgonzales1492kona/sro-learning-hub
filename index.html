Amendment IV

Search, seizure, and the school setting

SRO priority

The Fourth Amendment addresses unreasonable government searches and seizures. In schools, a key starting point is identifying who is acting, for what purpose, and whether the contact restricts a student’s freedom.

Core questions

Is this a school-official action, law-enforcement action, or a joint effort? Is there a search or seizure? What is the relevant standard under current law and policy?

High-value SRO topics

Private versus government search; school official versus officer; lockers and personal effects; K9s; metal detectors; common authority; consensual contacts; reasonable suspicion; and probable cause.

Study takeaway

Do not assume that a school setting removes constitutional analysis. Record the facts, identify the role and purpose, and verify the governing authority.

Amendment V

Questioning, Miranda, and due process

Interview focus

The Fifth Amendment includes protection against compelled self-incrimination and due process protections. For interview analysis, focus on custody, interrogation, voluntariness, and youth-specific considerations.

Core questions

Is the student in custody for Miranda purposes? Is there interrogation or its functional equivalent? Has the student invoked a right, and what does current law require next?

High-value SRO topics

School questioning versus law-enforcement questioning; custodial setting; Miranda warnings; invocation; waiver; voluntariness; parent or guardian issues; and documentation.

Study takeaway

“At school” does not answer the custody question. Analyze the total setting and use your jurisdiction’s current juvenile-interview rules.

Amendment VI

Right to counsel and criminal prosecution

Case-status focus

The Sixth Amendment protects rights in criminal prosecutions, including counsel, confrontation, speedy trial, and an impartial jury. The right-to-counsel analysis differs from Fifth Amendment/Miranda analysis.

Core questions

Has adversarial judicial process begun on a particular offense? Is the contact related to that charged offense? Is a separate Fifth Amendment/Miranda issue also present?

High-value SRO topics

When the Sixth Amendment right attaches; charged versus uncharged conduct; counsel requests; the difference between Sixth Amendment counsel and Miranda counsel; and referral coordination.

Study takeaway

Keep the amendments distinct: Miranda custody/interrogation analysis and post-charge right-to-counsel analysis are related but not interchangeable.
